Shawn Zheng is a Washington, D.C.-based hornist who enjoys a diverse career as an orchestral and chamber musician, soloist, and educator. He currently serves as a member of "The President's Own" U.S. Marine Band.

 

He was a winner of the National YoungArts Foundation competition, prizewinner of the Nashville Symphony Concerto Competition, Curb Youth Symphony Competition, and Southeast Horn Symposium Competition, and made his solo concerto debut with “The President’s Own” U.S. Marine Band as the winner of its high school concerto competition. He has performed as a guest musician with the Houston Symphony, Houston Ballet, Richmond Symphony, and Washington National Opera, among others. His broad musical interests have brought him to perform in Rice’s new music ensemble Hear&Now, as part of the Ziering-Conlon Initiative for Recovered Voices, featuring music by Jewish composers whose lives and careers were disrupted during the years of the Nazi regime, and as a recurring guest artist with the Colburn Chamber Music Society. In previous summers, he was a Fellow of the National Orchestral Institute, the Sarasota Music Festival, and the National Youth Orchestra of the USA, performing in Carnegie Hall, at the Tanglewood Music Festival, and across Latin America.

 

A devoted educator and proponent of community engagement, he has taught masterclasses at Western Michigan University, for the Colburn Community School and Jumpstart Young Musician’s Program, and given numerous clinics across the country. He was also a contributor to Rice University’s Just For U Music Program (JUMP!), a student-led initiative that produces educational performances for Houston students. During the pandemic, Shawn founded and directed the Hanszen Chamber Music Festival, a genre-spanning concert series that sought to create valuable concertizing experiences for both performers and audiences. 

 

He received his Bachelor of Music from Rice University’s Shepherd School of Music, where he studied with William VerMeulen, and pursued post-graduate studies at The Colburn School with Andrew Bain. He also remains grateful for numerous other mentors, including Leslie Norton at Vanderbilt University’s Blair Academy, and Corbin Wagner at the Interlochen Arts Academy.
