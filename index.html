Shawn Zheng
performing teaching contact

Shawn Zheng is a Chinese-American musician and educator, and is the recently appointed Principal Horn of The U.S. Army Band “Pershing’s Own,” located in the Washington, D.C. area, where he performs for the public and in federal state functions. A regular guest musician with orchestra across the country, he has performed with the Baltimore Symphony, Houston Symphony, Los Angeles Philharmonic, National Symphony, Nashville Symphony, Richmond Symphony, and Rochester Philharmonic.

 

He is a National YoungArts Foundation Winner, and made his concerto debut with “The President’s Own” U.S. Marine Band as the winner of its solo competition. He has also been a prizewinner of the Nashville Symphony Concerto Competition, Curb Youth Symphony Competition. During previous summers, he was a Fellow of the National Orchestral Institute, the Sarasota Music Festival, and the National Youth Orchestra of the USA.

 

A devoted educator and proponent of community engagement, he has taught sectionals for the Colburn Community School and Jumpstart Young Musician’s Program, a tuition-free program that provides opportunities for low-income Los Angeles-area students. He was also a contributor to Rice University’s Just For U Music Program (JUMP!), a student-led initiative that produces educational performances for Houston students. During the pandemic, Shawn founded and directed the Hanszen Chamber Music Festival, a genre-spanning concert series that sought to create valuable concertizing experiences for both performers and audiences. 

 

He received his Bachelor of Music from Rice University’s Shepherd School of Music, where he studied with William VerMeulen, and was awarded the Professional Studies Certificate from the Colburn School studying with Andrew Bain. Born and raised in Tennessee, he has also studied with Leslie Norton at Vanderbilt University’s Blair Academy, and attended the Interlochen Arts Academy, where he studied with Corbin Wagner. In his free time, he enjoys ceramics, weight training, and reading.
